# 1:1 沟通准备示例 (One-on-One Prep Examples)

## 示例一：和直属上级的资源讨论

### 用户输入

```text
帮我准备一下明天和老板的 1:1。

我最近最重要的几件事：
- 会员系统重构做到 80%
- 带小李独立负责消息中心，已经上线了 2 个功能
- Q2 规划文档写完了

现在最大问题是前端人手不够，小王下周开始休假两周，会员系统前端部分可能会拖慢。
我想在 1:1 里争取一个外包前端，或者让老板帮我调整优先级。
时间大概 30 分钟。
```

### ManageUp 输出

```markdown
# 与直属上级的 1:1 准备

## 本次目标

在 30 分钟内先对齐会员系统的人力风险，并争取资源或优先级调整。

## 优先讨论事项

1. **会员系统前端人力风险**
   - 现状：会员系统重构当前完成约 80%，但小王下周开始休假 2 周，前端交付风险上升
   - 为什么重要：如果没有额外支持，前端部分可能拖慢整体上线节奏
   - 我希望得到的帮助：确认是否可以引入外包前端，或明确调整项目优先级

2. **新人培养进展**
   - 现状：小李已独立负责消息中心，并已上线 2 个功能
   - 为什么重要：这说明团队培养有效，但短期还不能完全覆盖前端缺口
   - 我希望得到的帮助：确认后续是否继续给小李更大的 ownership

3. **Q2 规划落地优先级**
   - 现状：Q2 规划文档已完成
   - 为什么重要：如果会员系统延期，Q2 规划中的后续事项需要重排
   - 我希望得到的帮助：请老板确认下个月的优先级取舍

## 建议提问

- 如果只能保一个目标，您更希望我保住会员系统上线时间，还是保住 Q2 新项目启动时间？
- 如果短期不能补人，您建议我优先砍掉哪些低价值需求？

## 如果时间不够，至少要讲这三句

- 会员系统当前完成 80%，但前端人力风险会影响后续节奏
- 小王下周休假 2 周，我需要资源补位或优先级调整
- 希望这次 1:1 结束前明确一个处理方向

## 会后跟进

- 根据 1:1 结论更新项目排期和资源方案
```

## Example 2: Skip-level career conversation

### User Input

```text
Help me prepare for a skip-level 1:1 with the VP.

What matters most:
- I led the onboarding workflow redesign and reduced drop-off from 42% to 31%
- I also mentored a new PM who now runs the weekly experiment review
- The product-engineering prioritization process still changes too often, and it creates rework

What I want from the meeting:
- Get feedback on whether I am operating at senior PM level
- Ask what gaps I need to close for promotion

I want this to be concise and professional.
```

### ManageUp Output

```markdown
# 1:1 Prep with VP

## Goal for This Meeting

Get clear feedback on whether my current scope is operating at senior PM level and what gaps I should close for promotion.

## Priority Topics

1. **Recent impact**
   - Current state: I led the onboarding workflow redesign and reduced drop-off from 42% to 31%
   - Why it matters: this shows product impact on a core funnel metric
   - What I need: feedback on how this level of ownership is viewed at the VP level

2. **Team leverage**
   - Current state: I mentored a new PM who now runs the weekly experiment review
   - Why it matters: this shows leverage beyond individual execution
   - What I need: perspective on whether this is the kind of leadership expected for promotion

3. **Prioritization instability**
   - Current state: product-engineering priorities change too often, creating rework
   - Why it matters: it slows execution and makes planning less reliable
   - What I need: advice on how to escalate or improve this without creating friction

## Questions to Ask

- Based on my current scope and results, where do you see me relative to senior PM expectations?
- What are the top one or two gaps I should close over the next 6 months to be promotion-ready?

## If Time Is Tight, Say These Three Things

- I want candid feedback on whether my current work is at senior PM level
- My recent impact includes reducing onboarding drop-off from 42% to 31% and mentoring a PM to own experiment review
- I would like your advice on the biggest gap to close next

## Follow-up

- Capture promotion expectations and convert them into a 3-6 month development plan
```
